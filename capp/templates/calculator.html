Carbon Emissions Calculation Formula:

Carbon Emissions (kg CO₂) = Distance Traveled (km) × Emission Factor (g CO₂/km) / amout of passengers

Car

The carbon emissions from driving depend on your vehicle's fuel type. Diesel cars produce 229 g CO2/km, while petrol cars emit 198 g CO2/km. Electric vehicles are the greenest option, producing just 59 g CO2/km roughly one-third the emissions of petrol and one-quarter of diesel vehicles.

Airplane

Economy class produces 127 g CO2/pkm while business class generates 284 g CO2/pkm Economy is significantly more eco-friendly since emissions are shared among more passengers, whereas business class has fewer passengers per plane, resulting in a higher carbon footprint per person.

Train

Train travel is one of the most eco-friendly transportation options. Electric trains produce just 7 g CO2/pkm while diesel trains emit 91 g CO2/pkm. Electric trains are significantly cleaner and represent the greenest way to travel long distances.

Ferry

Boat emissions vary significantly based on capacity. Passenger boats produce 186 g CO2/pkm, making them a moderate option for water travel. Cars transported without passengers emit 377 g CO2/pkm more than double the emissions of passenger boats.

Bus

Diesel buses emit 30g of CO₂ per kilometer consider greener options to keep your travel more sustainable!

Bicycle

This mode of transport produces no CO₂ during use the perfect choice for 100% sustainable travel.
